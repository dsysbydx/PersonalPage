About Me

I am currently a sixth year PhD student in the Department of Mathematics, Stanford University working under the supervision of Professor George C. Papanicolaou. I was also in the Financial Mathematics program where I completed a master degree in 2012. Before coming to Stanford, I went to Brown University for my undergraduate study where I majored in mathematics and applied mathematics-economics.

Research

My general research interest lies in the area of applied probability, stochastic analysis, and their applications. Currently, my work has been focused on a type of model called mean field games (MFG) which describe the limit of large population stochastic differential games with symmetric and mean-field properties.

  • Wellposedness of Mean Field Games with Common Noise under a Weak Monotonicity Condition (Submitted) [arXiv]
  • Asymptotic Analysis of Mean Field Games with Small Common Noise (In Preparation)
